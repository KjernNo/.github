# Welcome to Kjern! :)

You should know what you buy. You should own it, understand it, and have control over it. That's not a feature. That's just how it should work.

Too many companies today treat customers as a revenue line on a slide deck. Decisions get made to please shareholders, not the people actually using the product. The money comes first, the customer comes second, and somewhere along the way the whole point gets lost.

We think that's wrong. And we're building Kjern to be the opposite of it.

---

Kjern is a small Norwegian electronics company making consumer and professional hardware. Founded and run by Phillip Rødseth, and intentionally kept independent. No outside investors pulling strings. No board making decisions based on quarterly numbers. When Kjern eventually becomes an AS, the plan has always been to hold the majority of shares and keep it that way, so the company never ends up in a situation where money wins over doing right by the customer.

Customer relations come before everything else here. That's not a policy, it's just the whole point.

---

## Open source

We open source our firmware, and sometimes our hardware too. Schematics, Gerber files, PCB layouts. Not because we have to, but because we think you should be able to see what's running on the thing you bought. Transparency isn't a marketing word at Kjern. It's how we operate.

This GitHub is where that lives.

## Projects

### [SPECTER](https://github.com/KjernNo/specter)

A handheld 2.4GHz spectrum analyzer, BLE monitor and WiFi scanner built on a Raspberry Pi Pico W. Completely custom MicroPython firmware, hand soldered on perfboard. Open source under CERN-OHL-W-2.0.
